FAA Rock Slope Kinematic Analysis — Figure Captions
====================================================

These figures were produced from the bundled sample data (sample_slope.xyzn)
using the FAA algorithms with: slope dip 70 deg, dip direction 140 deg,
friction angle 30 deg, lateral limits 20 deg (sliding & toppling), minimum
angular difference 30 deg, and k = 16 nearest neighbours.

Result counts: 900 sliding points, 720 toppling points, 826 wedge intersections.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
fig1_stereonet.png
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Figure 1. Lower-hemisphere, equal-angle stereonet of the kinematic
analysis. Each point is the pole to a discontinuity surface. Red poles
satisfy the planar-sliding criteria (daylighting, friction-cone and
lateral-limit tests); green poles satisfy the flexural-toppling criteria.
Grey poles are kinematically stable. The white ring marks the pole to the
design slope face (dip 70 deg, dip direction 140 deg).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
fig2_3dview.png
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Figure 2. Three-dimensional view of the analysed rock slope, coloured by
kinematic failure mode: planar sliding (red), flexural toppling (green)
and wedge sliding (cyan) over the stable rock face (grey). Axes are in
metres (x = East, y = North, z = Up). Design slope 70 deg / 140 deg,
friction angle 30 deg.
